# Privacy-First Data Architecture

Verified: 2026-05-29

This page is an implementation guardrail, not legal advice or a HIPAA compliance certification. It defines how MassageLab separates client-owned wellness tools, therapist professional records, and future sharing workflows while hosted professional-record storage remains gated.

## Architecture Position

MassageLab should not treat every health-adjacent feature as one shared clinical record. The product has three storage/control zones plus two bridge interfaces:

| Domain | Owner | Storage posture | Current product rule |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `AccountProfile` | User or practice | Cloud-safe app storage | Name, email, phone, login, profile, preferences, membership, and booking/account status can use the normal app database with least-privilege access. |
| `ClientWellnessRecord` | Client | Future cloud wellness storage with strong consumer-health privacy controls | Client self-tracking can become cloud-backed later, but therapist access is not automatic and no client wellness data should be sold, used for ad targeting, or repurposed away from the user's benefit. |
| `ProfessionalRecordVault` | Therapist or practice | Offline-capable local encrypted storage until hosted PHI gates pass | Therapist-created intake, SOAP, treatment, pain-map, signature, and professional-note content must not be stored, processed, logged, emailed, or synced through MassageLab cloud services yet. |
| `SharingConsent` | Client | Future cloud consent/control record | Sharing is modeled now but live therapist viewing of cloud wellness data is deferred until legal/compliance review and explicit product gates exist. |
| `ProfessionalRecordReference` | Therapist or practice | Local professional vault first; hosted only after PHI gates | If a therapist uses client-shared wellness information in treatment planning or notes, the reference becomes an intentional professional-record entry. |

## Domain Rules

### Account, Contact, And Booking Data

Account/contact/booking data can use the existing cloud app stack when it is limited to identity, contact, account, membership, scheduling, and booking status. Scheduling metadata can still reveal a care or wellness relationship, so APIs should keep role checks, ownership checks, audit intent, and minimal response shapes.

Pre-arrival remote flows stay contact/profile/booking-only. They must not collect health history, contraindications, pain maps, intake answers, signatures, treatment notes, or professional clinical documents.

### Client-Owned Wellness Data

Client self-tracking is allowed as a future cloud domain, separate from therapist professional records. Examples include ROM self-measurements, body comfort trends, goals, home-care logs, sleep/stress notes, and client-authored wellness journals.

This domain should be treated as sensitive consumer health data even when it is not HIPAA PHI by default. Before cloud wellness storage ships, it needs:

- explicit product language that it is self-tracking, not diagnosis or continuous monitoring
- export and deletion controls
- data category labels
- privacy settings
- no sale, ad-targeting, or unrelated secondary use of user health data
- logging and analytics rules that prevent raw wellness entries from leaking into third-party tools

Therapists must not receive live remote access to this data in the current alpha. The data may be modeled for future sharing, but active therapist viewing remains disabled until the sharing gate below is implemented.

### Therapist Professional Records

Therapist-created records are the highest-risk domain. This includes intake forms reviewed or generated by the therapist, SOAP notes, pain/body maps used for treatment, signatures, contraindication screening, treatment plans, professional observations, and session notes.

Current rule: keep these records local-first and out of MassageLab cloud services. They must not be sent to Prisma, hosted API routes, account preferences, Sentry/error logs, analytics, email providers, backup queues, or clinical sync endpoints.

The target local storage design is an offline `ProfessionalRecordVault`:

- passphrase-based unlock that works without network access
- encryption before records are persisted
- session-only unlock by default
- no server-side password recovery for the first vault version
- wrong-passphrase rejection without exposing plaintext
- explicit export/import flows for backup and transfer

SOAP, intake, journal, and ROM now use this shared vault model in browser storage under `massagelab-professional-record-vault-v1`. Encrypted `.mlab` full-vault bundles are the normal transfer and backup format; DOC/PDF output is available only after an explicit plaintext-output warning. Encrypted browser storage and encrypted export bundles do not replace device passcodes, OS disk encryption, browser profile security, backups, staff policies, or incident-response procedures.

### Future Sharing Bridge

The future bridge should be consent-based, not automatic. A client may eventually grant a therapist access to selected wellness categories, selected entries, summary trends, or continuous future entries. That permission should be represented by `SharingConsent` with scope, granted time, revoked time, expiration if any, and audit metadata.

Therapist use should be deliberate. Viewing a client-shared wellness trend is not the same as importing it into the professional record. A therapist should intentionally create a `ProfessionalRecordReference` when they rely on shared wellness data in treatment planning, SOAP notes, or other professional documentation.

Live therapist dashboards for cloud wellness sharing are deferred. Manual export/review and local-vault import are safer intermediate steps if the product needs therapist review before hosted professional storage is approved.

## Hosted PHI Gate

Hosted professional-record storage remains disabled until the compliance gate is intentionally opened. The existing clinical sync gate stays the hard implementation boundary:

```text
MASSAGELAB_ENABLE_HOSTED_PHI_SYNC=true
MASSAGELAB_HIPAA_BAA_CONFIRMED=true
MASSAGELAB_HIPAA_RISK_REVIEW_CONFIRMED=true
```

Those flags are necessary but not sufficient. Hosted professional-record storage also needs legal review, vendor BAAs, access controls, audit logs, backup and retention rules, breach/incident response, PHI-safe logging and email rules, support procedures, and operational policies.

Encryption alone does not remove the need for a BAA when a cloud provider maintains ePHI on behalf of a covered entity or business associate. HHS cloud guidance says a cloud service provider maintaining encrypted ePHI can still be a business associate even if it cannot decrypt the data.

## Vendor Notes

These notes are current source checks for architecture planning, not procurement decisions:

- HHS states HIPAA applies to covered entities and business associates, and entities outside those definitions do not have HIPAA obligations solely because data is health-related.
- HHS health-app guidance distinguishes consumer-selected apps from apps provided by or on behalf of a covered entity.
- FTC guidance says its Health Breach Notification Rule can apply to non-HIPAA health apps, connected devices, and similar products when unsecured, individually identifiable health information is breached.
- Vercel's Pro plan documentation listed a HIPAA BAA add-on for healthcare compliance agreements at `$350/month` when this page was verified.
- Cloudflare's US privacy compliance page says Cloudflare only enters BAAs with enterprise customers.

Re-verify vendor pricing, BAA availability, and product scope before any hosted professional-record decision. Vendor pages and plan terms can change.

## Implementation Guardrails

- Keep `/notes/intake`, SOAP, journal, ROM, and future therapist documentation routes local-first unless the hosted PHI gate passes.
- Keep browser QA and source-guard tests that block clinical content from `/api/clinical/sync`, `/api/clients/`, `/api/account/preferences`, Prisma writes, logs, analytics, and other hosted routes.
- Do not expose remote client wellness dashboards to therapists until `SharingConsent` has been designed, reviewed, tested, and intentionally enabled.
- When encrypted local vault behavior is added to a professional-record route, test offline unlock, wrong-passphrase rejection, session-only unlock, export/import, no plaintext persistence, and malformed import rejection.
- Public and client-facing copy should avoid diagnosis, emergency monitoring, continuous therapist monitoring, or medical-device style claims.
